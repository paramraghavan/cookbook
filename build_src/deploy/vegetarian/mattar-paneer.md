# Matar Paneer

## Ingredients  
*(1 cup = 250 ml)*

### Main Ingredients for Matar Paneer
- 250 to 300 grams paneer (cottage cheese)  
- 1 cup peas or matar – fresh or frozen  
- 1 to 1.25 cups water **OR** 250 to 315 ml water (add as required)  
- ½ teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera)  
- ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder (haldi)  
- ¼ to ½ teaspoon red chili powder (lal mirch powder)  
- ¼ to ½ teaspoon garam masala powder or Punjabi garam masala  
- 1 tablespoon malai or cream (optional)  
- ½ teaspoon sugar or add as required (optional)  
- 2 to 2.5 tablespoons oil or ghee  
- A few coriander leaves (dhania) for garnishing  
- Salt as required  

---

### Ingredients for Masala Paste
- 3 medium ripe tomatoes – roughly chopped **OR** ¾ cup roughly chopped tomatoes  
- 1 medium to large onion – roughly chopped **OR** ½ cup roughly chopped onions  
- 1 to 2 green chilies (hari mirch) – chopped  
- ½ inch ginger (adrak) – chopped  
- 2 to 3 garlic cloves (lahsun) – chopped  
- 2 tablespoons roughly chopped coriander leaves  
- 10 to 12 whole cashews (kaju) – chopped  
- 4 to 5 whole black peppercorns (sabut kali mirch)  
- 2 to 3 cloves (lavang)  
- ½ inch cinnamon stick (dalchini)  
- 1 teaspoon coriander seeds (sabut dhania)  

---

# How to Make Matar Paneer

## Make the Masala Paste
1. In a grinder or blender, add all ingredients listed under **“masala paste”**:  
   ½ cup chopped onions, ¾ cup chopped tomatoes, ½ inch ginger, 2–3 garlic cloves,  
   1–2 green chilies, 10–12 cashews, 2 tbsp coriander leaves, 4–5 peppercorns,  
   ½ inch cinnamon, 1 tsp coriander seeds, 2–3 cloves.  

2. Grind into a smooth paste.

3. Do **not** add water while grinding. Ensure there are **no bits** of cashews.

4. Set the paste aside.

---

## Make the Matar Paneer
1. Heat oil or ghee. Add ½ teaspoon cumin seeds and sauté until they splutter.  
2. Add the masala paste. Stir and sauté; it may splutter. Cover partially if needed.  
3. Sauté for 10–12 minutes on low to medium flame until the oil separates from the paste.  
4. Add dry spices — turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala. Mix well.  
5. If adding cream or malai, add now. Stir and sauté for a minute.  
6. Add peas (matar). Stir again.  
7. Add water and salt.  
8. Cover cooker and pressure cook **2 to 3 whistles** or **9–10 minutes**.  
9. When pressure drops, open the cooker.  
10. If curry is thin, simmer until medium consistency.  
11. If thick, add water and simmer.  
12. Add paneer cubes. Simmer 1–2 minutes on low flame until soft.  
    - Optional: Add a little sugar before adding paneer for slight sweetness.  
13. Do not overcook the paneer.  
14. Garnish with coriander leaves.  
15. **Serve hot** with rotis, parathas, naan, or rice.

# Vegetable Samosa

A crispy, golden pastry filled with spiced potatoes and peas - a classic Indian snack!

## Ingredients

### For the Dough
- 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
- 4 tablespoons oil
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- Water as needed

### For the Filling
- 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
- 1 cup green peas, boiled
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
- 2 green chilies, chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

## Instructions

### Prepare the Dough
1. Mix flour, salt, and oil in a large bowl
2. Add water gradually and knead into a stiff dough
3. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes

### Make the Filling
1. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds
2. Add ginger and green chilies, saut√© for a minute
3. Add peas, mashed potatoes, and all spices
4. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes
5. Add lemon juice and coriander leaves
6. Let the filling cool completely

### Shape and Fry
1. Divide dough into 8 equal balls
2. Roll each ball into an oval shape
3. Cut in half to make semi-circles
4. Form a cone shape and fill with potato mixture
5. Seal the edges with water
6. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown

## Cooking Time
- Prep: 30 minutes
- Rest: 30 minutes
- Cook: 30 minutes
- Total: 90 minutes

## Servings
Makes 16 samosas

## Tips
- Make sure the filling is completely cool before filling
- Keep the oil at medium-high heat for perfect crispiness
- Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce
- Can be frozen before frying and cooked later

## Storage
Store fried samosas in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Reheat in an oven for best results.

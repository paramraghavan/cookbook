### 1. Quad Sets

* **Visual Pose:** You will see the doctor or patient lying completely flat on their back on a bed with their legs extended straight forward. The posture focuses strictly on the thigh muscle contracting hard to push the back of the knee down against the surface.
* **See the image/demonstration at:** **[[01:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwbAZSYLvYY&t=93)]**

### 2. Straight Leg Raise (SLR)

* **Visual Pose:** While lying on the back, one leg is bent at a 90-degree angle with the foot flat on the bed to stabilize the pelvis. The other leg is perfectly straight and lifted parallel to the opposite knee, just about 4 to 5 inches off the mattress.
* **See the image/demonstration at:** **[[02:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwbAZSYLvYY&t=131)]**

### 3. Mini Squats (Half Squats)

* **Visual Pose:** The posture shows a person standing upright, holding onto a solid counter or chair back. The feet are shoulder-width apart, and the hips shift backward slightly (as if sitting in a high chair) without letting the knees bend past a shallow angle.
* **See the image/demonstration at:** **[[02:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwbAZSYLvYY&t=160)]**

### 4. Standing Leg Curl

* **Visual Pose:** Standing straight and holding onto a counter for balance. One leg stays firmly planted on the ground while the other knee bends backward, bringing the heel up toward the glutes while the thighs stay perfectly aligned side-by-side.
* **See the image/demonstration at:** **[[03:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwbAZSYLvYY&t=192)]**

### 5. Bridging

* **Visual Pose:** Lying flat on the back with both knees bent and feet flat on the bed. The posture shows the hips and lower back lifted off the bed to create a straight diagonal line from the shoulders up to the knees.
* **See the image/demonstration at:** **[[03:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwbAZSYLvYY&t=218)]**
